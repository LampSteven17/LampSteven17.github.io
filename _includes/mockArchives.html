Spikey Lemonade

A riff on the Tom Collins, I wanted a more summer themed recipie that utilized tequila instead of a traditional london dry gin. I used a premixed lemonade bought fresh at the local grocery store, but you can do this recipie with straight lemon juice (it will be very sour so just add sweetener to taste). The seltzer water adds a nice fizziness and spices the drink up from a simple lemonade screwdriver. Recipie should be simple and easy to make.

Cocktail

  • 1.00 oz. Reposado Tequila
  • 1.25 oz. Lemonade (or lemon juice)
  • 0.75 oz. Seltzer Water
  • Squeze of lime juice in glass

Mocktail

  • 2.00 oz. Lemonade (or lemon juice)
  • 1.00 oz. Seltzer Water
  • Dash of Citrus Bitters
  • Squeze of lime juice in glass

Piña Colada

Potentially invented by bartender Ramón “Monchito” Marrero, 1954. The Pina Colada is a classic tiki drink, universally praised for its simplicity in taste and requirments to make. The drink traditionally consists of more than one type of rum, to give the apperance of being exotic (signature move of Don Beach). Here is my "what was in my fridge at the time" attempt to replicate this drink.

Cocktail

  • 1.00 oz. Light Rum
  • 2.25 oz. Pineapple Juice
  • 1.50 oz. Coconut Cream (Jigger Overflowing)
  • 0.50 oz. Orange Juice (Or other citrus such as lime/lemon juice)

Mocktail

  • 3.00 oz. Pineapple Juice
  • 1.50 oz. Coconut Cream (Jigger Overflowing)
  • 1.00 oz. Orange Juice (Or other citrus such as lime/lemon juice)
